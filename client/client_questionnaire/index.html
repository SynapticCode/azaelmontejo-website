The Basics
Physical Activity Readiness Questionnaire (PAR-Q)

IMPORTANT: Please read the questions carefully and answer each one honestly. Check "YES" or "NO" for each question.

1. Has your doctor ever said that you have a heart condition and that you should only do physical activity recommended by a doctor?

2. Do you feel pain in your chest when you do physical activity?

3. In the past month, have you had chest pain when you were not doing physical activity?

4. Do you lose your balance because of dizziness or do you ever lose consciousness?

5. Do you have a bone or joint problem (for example, back, knee, or hip) that could be made worse by a change in your physical activity?

6. Is your doctor currently prescribing drugs (for example, water pills) for your blood pressure or heart condition?

7. Do you know of any other reason why you should not do physical activity?

PAR-Q Clearance: If you answered "YES" to one or more questions, please consult your doctor before starting any exercise program. If you answered "NO" to all questions, you can be reasonably sure that you can safely participate in physical activity.

Part I: Your Vision & Motivation

If you could achieve only ONE thing in the next 3-6 months, what would it be? Be as specific as possible.

What other outcomes are you hoping for? Think about aesthetics (how you look), performance (what you can do), and health (how you feel).

What is the deep, personal reason driving you to make this change now? What will achieving these goals make possible for you?

Part II: Your Physical Landscape

Describe any areas of your body that are currently causing you discomfort. Where is it, what does it feel like (sharp, dull, achy), and what activities make it better or worse?

Please list any significant past injuries, surgeries, or conditions, even if they feel "resolved."

Are you currently being treated for any medical conditions (e.g., high blood pressure, diabetes, thyroid issues, etc.) or taking any medications that might affect your training?

Part III: Your Training Style

Briefly describe your history with exercise. What have you tried in the past? What did you love? What did you dislike?

What kind of physical activity feels like "play" to you? Conversely, what feels like a "chore"?

Part IV: Your Daily Life

List every single piece of equipment you have consistent access to. Be specific with weights if possible.

On average, how many hours of sleep do you get per night? How would you rate the quality of your sleep (1=Awful, 10=Perfect)?

Briefly describe your typical daily eating habits. What are your biggest nutritional challenges?

What are the main sources of stress in your life? What do you currently do to manage stress and recover (mentally or physically)?
